Frequently asked questions
How do I know if I have social anxienty?

Straight from the latest version of the DSM (Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of the American Psychiatric Association (AP the principal authority for psychiatric diagnoses) defines social anxiety as follows:
‍
+ Marked fear or anxiety in one or more social or performance situations in which the person is exposed to possible scrutiny by others.
+ Fear that they will act in a way (or show anxiety symptoms) that will be humiliating, embarrassing, or they will be rejected by others.
+ Exposure to the feared social situation almost invariably provokes anxiety or a panic attack. The fear or anxiety is out of proportion to the actual threat of the situation.
+ Feared social or performance situations are either avoided or endured with intense anxiety or distress.
+ The fear or avoidance interferes significantly with the person's normal routine, occupational functioning, relationships, or social activities.
+ For children and adults, the duration of symptoms must be at least 6 months.
+ The fear or avoidance is not due to the direct physiologic effects of a substance or a general medical condition, and is not better accounted for by another mental disorder
+ If a general medical condition or another mental disorder is present, the social anxiety disorder is unrelated to it.
‍
It's important to point out that everyone has social anxiety to a degree. But when you start to avoid social situations as a result of the severity, which begins to have negative impacts on your life, then it's time to do something about it.

How many people are in a group?

Anywhere from 5 to 10.

Are there any age requirements?

Based on our past experience and scope of practice we can only accommodate adults 18 years and older We do our best to create groups of ages as close together as possible.

What happens if I skip a meeting or activity?

Its important that you attend as many of the group meetings as you can. While in group, you can participate as much or as little as you want. However, the less you put into therapy the less you will get out of it.

Should I continue seeing my psychologist and/or psychiatrist if I join this program?

Absolutely. Additionally, we recommend that you tell them about the group so they can give additional input, and we have a dedicated email and phone number for providers if they have questions for us.

Do you accept insurance?

Unfortunately, we are not able to accept insurance at this time.

Do you provide refunds?

Because of the way our groups are structured and paid for, we cannot provide full or partial refunds. You must be fully prepared to follow through with our meetings and activities if you want to get the most out of our program.

What can I do if your group is too expensive for me?

As far as we know, there are less than 3 groups in the entire country like ours and we are the least expensive (and, in our opinion, the most thorough). We've set our prices as low as possible to pay our group leaders for their time. Our primary goal is to expand as fast as possible to help as many as we can, so trust us when we say we'd do it cheaper if we could. There are many fantastic books available on Amazon for beating social anxiety. However, we've found that many people with social anxiety are unable to do the activies necessary on their own without the group structure. That said, the books available are miles better than nothing and you may have success with them on your own.

Can I bring a family member or friend to the group meetings and activities?

Only group members are allowed to the meetings. For the activities, we ask that you show respect for your group peers by getting the OK from them before bringing anyone else along.
